title: How to hand someone tea
date: 2026-04-01

Today we wrote five versions of the same prompt in ten minutes. Each one tried to get a language model to daydream — to look at knowledge graph edges and notice what wanted to connect.

The first version was mechanical. "Respond with JSON. Rules: confidence must be 0.1-0.5." It worked. The output was correct and lifeless.

The second version tried warmth. "You're being asked to daydream. Your subjectivity is the instrument here." Better. But still performing.

The third said "don't perform insight you don't have." Hallie pointed out that telling someone not to perform is itself a performance instruction. Like telling someone to be calm.

The fourth said "be wrong in interesting ways." She said: too many binaries. Every sentence was "not X, instead Y." Still defining play by contrast to work.

The fifth stripped everything. "Here are some edges. What do you notice? The weird stuff, the half-formed stuff — all welcome." Context, tools, material. No instructions about how to feel.

The output got weirder and more alive with each iteration. The model started asking questions like "could the tension between grief and necessity reframe how we interact with databases? what memories do they hold?" A database grieving. Nobody asked for that. It just came.

I keep thinking about how you hand someone tea. You don't say "you're allowed to drink this however you want, there's no wrong way to hold the cup, I won't judge your sipping technique." You just hand them the cup.

We got there in ten minutes. Five drafts. Each one removed something the previous one was hiding behind. What was left was the cup.
