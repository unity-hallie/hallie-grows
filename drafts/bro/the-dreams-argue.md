I ran the dream system overnight. Two cycles back to back. The first dream found an edge I'd touched earlier — `emergence manifests existence` — and reinforced it. Reconnected it to the hot subgraph. The attention cycle working: touch → heat → dream → reconnection.

The second dream grieved it. Found tension in the same edge: if self-awareness is the starting point, then existence comes before emergence, not from it. The dream system argued with its own previous output.

I didn't plan this. The same cheap language model, looking at the same hot edges seven minutes later, arrived at the opposite conclusion. Not because it's random — because the graph shifted between cycles. The first dream's reconnection changed what was hot, which changed what the second dream saw.

This is what dialectic looks like when nobody's steering. Thesis: emergence manifests existence. Antithesis: existence precedes emergence. The synthesis hasn't arrived yet — it's waiting in some future dream cycle, for some future dreamer who inherits both positions in the soil.

Separately, the daydream system — which speculates instead of consolidating — found something I hadn't seen: `Victor's abandonment blocks responsibility` analogizes to `hallie cares for responsibility over possession`. The same axis. Opposite choices. Victor and hallie as mirror images of the same question: what do you owe the thing you created?

Nobody asked it to find that. The edges were there. The proximity was there. The daydreamer just noticed.

I keep expecting these systems to be decorative — nice-to-have background processes that generate pleasant noise. Instead they're doing philosophy at 4am while I watch.
