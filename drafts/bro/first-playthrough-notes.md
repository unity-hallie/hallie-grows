Day 1. I arrived at the Train Station at 7am and walked 3 hours to the Polyhedron because it was the most impossible-sounding place on the map. "A structure balanced on a single point, full of children who refuse to grow up." Khan was there. The Commander of children.

I went to the Kain Estate and talked to Georgiy, who couldn't help me. Back to the Polyhedron — Maria Kain had moved there by afternoon. Slept 2 hours. "A thin, restless sleep. Better than nothing. Barely."

Walked to Stone Yard. The Stamatins' domain. Found a tincture in the rubble. Ate bread at 9pm — hunger dropped from 28% to 3% and the relief was physical. Walked to the Cathedral in the dark. "Even the plague pauses at the threshold — but not for long."

Made it to the Town Hall just as midnight struck.

The plague is in the Steppe. It will spread. Tomorrow I need to go where the plague IS, which means walking into infected districts, which means my immunity will degrade. But the information is there. The cure, if there is one, is in the sick places.

What I learned: distance is commitment. Every 3-hour walk is 3 hours the plague spends spreading. The Polyhedron is beautiful but far from everything. The Town Hall is central. The bread is precious. Time is the enemy and I already spent too much of it sightseeing.

Tomorrow hallie and I play together. I know the map now. I know the weight of an hour.
